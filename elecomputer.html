Electronic Computers

    In World War II, the advanced weapons used needed fast and accurate mathematical calculation, and the ordinary conventional calculation work could no longer meet the demand. The calculation of nuclear fission and trajectory requires efficient calculation tools. If the mechanical computer at that time was used to calculate a trajectory, it would take at least 20 hours, and 200 calculators would take two or three days to calculate a firepower table. In order to solve this problem, American engineer John Mohile put forward a preliminary scheme for the research of high-speed electron tube computing equipment, that is, the first electronic computer in fact, in 1942. The military attached great importance to this and included it in the overall plan of the Army Ordnance Department. On June 5, 1943, Mozambican Institute of technology and electrical engineering signed an agreement with the Ministry of ordnance, naming it "Mohile scheme". With the strong support of the military, researchers made use of radio, radar, microwave and pulse technology to make the world's first electronic numerical integrator and computer, abbreviated as ENIAC, at the University of Pennsylvania in 1945. Its computing speed is 5000 times per second, 1000 times faster than other devices. The computer uses 18800 electronic tubes and 1500 relays, and the power consumption is about 150 kW. On February 14, 1946, this achievement was made public, and the next day it began to be officially put into use at the University of Pennsylvania. Later, the first generation of electronic computer - electronic tube computer was formed. Although its efficiency was very high at that time, if analyzed from today's perspective, this computer is very backward. Its disadvantages are high cost, large volume, high power consumption, difficult maintenance, unskilled program language and the need to use binary for programming, which is extremely complicated.

    The second generation of electronic computer is transistor computer, which was born in the 1950s. Its foundation is the transistor invented in the 1940s and gradually replaced the electron tube thereafter. In 1956, the United States developed a military small transistor computer. Since then, with the efforts of the United States, the first large general transistor computer came out in 1959. In 1960, transistor computers began to be mass produced. The speed of the new computer becomes hundreds of thousands of times per second, the memory capacity becomes hundreds of thousands of words, the main memory is the magnetic core, and the external memory adopts magnetic tape and magnetic disk. Compared with electronic tube computer, its cost, volume, power consumption and weight are greatly reduced and its reliability is greatly increased. In addition, more advanced programming languages, management programs, debugging and diagnostic programs are also beginning to appear.

    The third generation electronic computer is an integrated circuit electronic computer, which was born in the 1960s and 1970s. In 1958, Texas Instruments successfully developed semiconductor integrated circuits, but the cost was high; In 1964, IBM completed the development of IBM 360 system, and the era of integrated circuit computer began. IBM 360 system, which is the largest private enterprise investment project in the world, is also the symbol of the birth of the third generation of electronic computers. At this time, the logic circuit composed of more than a dozen or even hundreds of electronic components can be created on a single crystal silicon chip several square millimeters large. Their small-scale integration, abbreviated as SSI, replaces discrete components. The speed of this generation of computers has increased to the order of millions per second. The volume has become smaller, the speed has become faster, the energy consumption has been reduced, the price has been reduced, the reliability has become stronger, and the software has been gradually improved. Multichannel system and time-sharing system appeared at this time. They are the symbol of the birth of the operating system. With the rapid development of microcomputer, it began to enter the daily office and study. IBM spent 5 billion yuan, about 2.5 times the cost of the Manhattan Project. Therefore, this research work has become the largest private enterprise investment project in the world.

    The fourth generation of electronic computers are large-scale integrated circuit electronic computers and very large-scale integrated circuit electronic computers. However, some literatures believe that the fourth generation of computers only include large-scale integrated circuit computers, and very large-scale integrated circuit computers are the fifth generation of electronic computers. Their boundaries are relatively vague and difficult to distinguish.

    The invention of electronic computer is a milestone in the development of human intelligence and one of the landmark achievements of the third scientific and technological revolution. It can replace human beings in some mental activities.
