Nuclear Energy

Learn More...

Scientists in Germany and the United States are at the forefront of the world in the research of atomic energy. The University of Chicago has built the world's first nuclear reactor, and the manually controlled nuclear chain reaction has been successful. This is a historic breakthrough and opens the way for the successful development of the atomic bomb. In World War II, the atomic bomb was used in Japan for the first time in human history, killing and wounding a total of 140000 people. After World War II, the prospect of peaceful use of atomic energy was very broad, and nuclear power plants and nuclear medicine came into being. The use of atomic energy has also had a negative impact. During this period, nuclear leakage events such as Fukushima Daiichi nuclear power plant accident, Three Mile Island nuclear leakage accident and Chernobyl nuclear accident occurred.

Electronic Computers

Electronic Computers

Learn More...

With the support of the military, American researchers used radio, radar, microwave and pulse technology to make the world's first electronic computer at the University of Pennsylvania in 1945. The second generation of electronic computer is transistor computer, which was born in the 1950s. Its foundation is the transistor invented in the 1940s and gradually replaced the electron tube thereafter. The third generation electronic computer is an integrated circuit electronic computer, which was born in the 1960s and 1970s. The fourth generation of electronic computers are large-scale integrated circuit electronic computers and very large-scale integrated circuit electronic computers. The invention of electronic computer is a milestone in the development of human intelligence.

Bioengineering

Bioengineering

Learn More...

Bioengineering is a new comprehensive applied discipline that began to rise in the early 1970s. In the 1990s, bioengineering based on system theory, that is, the concept of system bioengineering, was born. Bioengineering is generally considered to be based on biological theories and technologies, combined with modern engineering technologies such as chemical industry, machinery and computer, make full use of the latest achievements of molecular biology, consciously manipulate genetic materials, directionally transform organisms or their functions, and create new species with super distant traits in a short time, It is an emerging technology to cultivate these "engineering bacteria" or "engineering cell lines" on a large scale through a suitable bioreactor in order to produce a large number of useful metabolites or give full play to their unique physiological functions.

The First industrial revolution

The first scientific and technological revolution is also called "the first industrial revolution" (1830s-the end of the 18th century) 1. Landmark achievements The first scientific and technological revolution took the reform of textile machinery as the starting point and the invention and use of steam engine as the symbol. The first scientific and technological revolution directly led to the Industrial Revolution (Industrial Revolution). Since then, mankind has entered the "factory age", "machine age" and "steam age". The greatest feature of the industrial revolution is that "it is manifested in the replacement of manpower by machines and manual production by individual workshops by large-scale industrial production. Great changes have taken place in productivity and production relations. It has brought human history into a new period." (quoted from) Wu Guosheng, the course of Science (Second Edition), Peking University Press, 2002 edition, page 257, "the arrival of the factory era has brought mankind into a real hurricane period in production." (quoted from a brief history of science and technology development compiled by Wang Shifang and Dong Zili, Peking University Press, 2005 edition, page 87) Replacing manpower with machines is the key to the industrial revolution. It is the invention and application of a large number of new machines, especially the invention, manufacture and use of "mule machine" spinning machine and steam power machine, which has greatly improved labor productivity and made industrial development by leaps and bounds. The starting country, Britain, has become the world's No. 1 power. The industrial revolution is a great change unmatched by the general political revolution. Its influence involves all aspects of human social life, makes great changes in human society, and plays an irreplaceable role in promoting the process of human modernization. Marx commented: "the steam king turned the whole world over in the previous century." 2. Iconic figures Watt, who improved the steam engine and produced the first two-way steam engine, was naturally a landmark figure in the first scientific and technological revolution. The steam engine was put into use in 1784, and its thermal efficiency was greatly improved. The coal consumption was only one third of that of the newcomer. This kind of steam engine was widely used in all walks of life and had a great impact in the first scientific and technological revolution. (the textile industry, mining industry, metallurgy industry and other industries are developing rapidly driven by Watt machines, and the machinery manufacturing industry is booming in order to manufacture watt machines.) In nearly a century, the steam engine set off and led the industrial revolution of the whole world, changed the economic basis of European capitalism, realized the transformation from handicraft industry to machine industry, created huge productivity, and created two classes: large industrial capitalists and industrial wage workers. (refer to page 91 of a brief history of science and technology development compiled by Wang Shifang and Dong Zili) It is worth mentioning that Watt is a particularly inspirational model. He was born into a working family and suffered from poverty and disease since childhood. As a teenager, he went to London as an apprentice and studied mechanical manufacturing. In 1756, he got a position as a mechanic at the University of Glasgow. In college, he met the famous physicist black and learned a lot of thermal knowledge from him. At the same time, he is actively thinking about how to improve the existing Newcomen steam engine (although widely used, the efficiency is still very low. It takes 25 kg of coal to produce one horsepower.) Because he was good at thinking and studying, he finally succeeded. In 1800, Watt was elected to the Royal Society, and the University of Glasgow awarded him an honorary doctorate.

The Second industrial revolution

Landmark achievements Marked by the invention of motor and the application of electric power, this "electric revolution" has pushed the era into the "electric era". Before the 19th century, people had a very limited understanding of electricity. Electricity was an unprecedented new type of energy faced by mankind at that time. The so-called "power revolution" refers to that the emerging electric energy began to dominate social and economic life as a main form of energy. The outstanding advantage of electric energy is that it is an industrial power that is easy to transmit. At the same time, it is an extremely effective and reliable information carrier. Therefore, the power revolution is mainly reflected in power transmission and information transmission. Associated with power transmission, there are large generators, high-voltage transmission network, various motors (motors) and lighting lamps. Associated with information transmission, telegraph, telephone and radio communication emerged. These great inventions have brought human life into a brighter and better new era. (quoted from Wu Guosheng, the course of Science (Second Edition), page 411) The social productive forces created in the electrical age are beyond the reach of the steam age. For example, in 1860, the industrial production of the United States only ranked fourth in the world, and the output value was only 10% of that of the capitalist world. Due to the extensive use of electricity, it increased by 9 in 1890, surpassing the British Empire and becoming the world's number one power. Power industry is still an important indicator of the development and application of the power industry. It is still an important sign of the whether power industry and life have been fundamentally changed. (quoted from page 140 of a brief history of science and technology development compiled by Wang Shifang and Dong Zili) Electrification was once regarded as the most ideal form of human life. The Soviet Union had a slogan: "Soviet plus electrification is communism", and China also regarded "upstairs and downstairs, electric lights and telephones" as the goal of struggle. In the United States and other developed countries, there are more and more household appliances, which facilitate people's life and reduce the intensity of housework. Large and small electrical appliances have entered thousands of households and decorated the cultural life of the new era. 2. Iconic figures Iconic figures include Edison, the American inventor, Siemens, the German scientist who invented the self-excited DC generator, dorivo dobrowski, the Russian engineer who realized the three-phase AC power transmission and the development of its generator, and Carl, the German engineer who made cars driven by internal combustion engines Benz, Morse, the inventor of the telegraph, bell, the inventor of the telephone, Marconi, the Italian engineer who realized transnational and transoceanic radio communication, Lafayette, the British physicist and chemist who laid the theoretical foundation for these technological inventions (discovered the principle of electromagnetic induction), Auster, the Danish physicist and chemist (discovered the magnetic effect of current in 1820), and others. In the 1960s and 1970s, German Siemens made generators and Belgian personality ram invented motors. Electricity began to be used to drive machines and become a new energy to supplement and replace steam power. The power industry and electrical appliance manufacturing industry have developed rapidly. The invention of motor realizes the exchange of electric energy and mechanical energy. Subsequently, electric lamps, trams, electric drills, electric welding machines and other electrical products sprung up. The invention of "three-phase AC transmission system and generator" by dorivo dobrowski marks the new beginning of the era of electrical technology. It not only solves the problem of long-distance transmission, but also makes electric energy more widely used in the industrial field. In the 1970s and 1980s, internal combustion engines fueled by gas and gasoline were born one after another, and the diesel engine was successfully created in the 1990s. The invention of the internal combustion engine solved the problem of the engine of the vehicle. In 1885, German Carl Benz successfully built the first car driven by internal combustion engine. Diesel locomotives, ocean going ships and aircraft have also developed rapidly. The invention of internal combustion engine also promoted the development of petroleum exploitation industry and the emergence of petrochemical industry.

The background of the third technological revolution

In the 1920s and 1930s, the theoretical system of quantum mechanics was established, which was combined with special relativity and laid the foundation for the emergence and development of nuclear energy (i.e. atomic energy). In 1931, the electron microscope was invented successfully. It can be magnified 10 times on the basis of the optical microscope, so that people can more intuitively understand the atomic world. From 1929 to 1933, British and American scientists invented the available cyclotron of high-speed protons. Electrostatic accelerator, high-frequency linear accelerator and electron induction accelerator also came out one after another, providing an effective means for the research of nuclear physics. In the 1930s, with the efforts of Otto Hahn and other Western scientists, the research on nuclear fission was successful, and human research on Atomic Energy achieved preliminary development. In 1926, John Logie Baird showed his television system for the first time. He was recognized as the inventor of television and known as the "father of television". The research of electronics has also made great progress. In 1920, radio broadcasting was born in the United States. In 1935, it basically covered all over the world. In 1925, Scotsman John Logie Baird successfully "scanned" the image in an experiment in London, which was the symbol of the birth of television. In 1937, the BBC began television broadcasting. In the same year, Britain invented a special military radar for detecting aircraft In addition, the Second World War, which broke out in 1939, was already brewing in the 1930s. Many countries are facing military crises, and the demand for advanced armaments with military advantages has also increased, and the needs of these wars have also directly or indirectly promoted the development of science and technology. Although the progress during this period is considerable, most of them are still in the stage of discussion and experiment, and have not been effectively practiced. The researchers themselves lack enough confidence and feel at a loss. Some people can't believe their achievements and even want to give up the distribution of relevant papers. Therefore, the revolution has not yet started.
